Top Exam Tips

1. Section choice matters! In Section A Q1, choose either (a) Prescribed Literature OR (b) Journalistic Text. Pick whichever you know best - don't waste time reading both.
2. Answer in the correct language. Read the question carefully - some require Spanish, some require English. Wrong language = zero marks.
3. Verbs must be correct for full marks. In written production (Section C), incorrect verb tenses will lose marks even if your message is clear.
4. Don't copy directly from texts. In Section B Q5 (opinion writing), phrases taken directly from the reading text earn no marks. Paraphrase!
5. Develop your points in written production. In letters/dialogues, each point should be expanded. Don't just give one-sentence answers.
6. Listen for key words. In the listening test, you hear each passage 3 times. First listen: get the gist. Second listen: write answers. Third listen: check details.
7. Write neatly! The paper is scanned. Anything outside the answer boxes may not be seen by the examiner.

Key Grammar Rules & Exam Phrases

Essential grammar points and useful exam phrases organised by topic.

Present Tense - Regular Verbs

-AR verbs (hablar)
hablo, hablas, habla, hablamos, habláis, hablan
-ER verbs (comer)
como, comes, come, comemos, coméis, comen
-IR verbs (vivir)
vivo, vives, vive, vivimos, vivís, viven

Key Irregular Verbs - Present Tense

ser (to be)
soy, eres, es, somos, sois, son
estar (to be)
estoy, estás, está, estamos, estáis, están
tener (to have)
tengo, tienes, tiene, tenemos, tenéis, tienen
ir (to go)
voy, vas, va, vamos, vais, van
hacer (to do/make)
hago, haces, hace, hacemos, hacéis, hacen

Preterite (Past) Tense - Regular Verbs

-AR verbs (hablar)
hablé, hablaste, habló, hablamos, hablasteis, hablaron
-ER/-IR verbs (comer/vivir)
comí/viví, comiste/viviste, comió/vivió, comimos/vivimos, comisteis/vivisteis, comieron/vivieron

Future Tense

All verbs use same endings
infinitive + é, ás, á, emos, éis, án

Example: hablaré, comeré, viviré

Conditional Tense

All verbs use same endings
infinitive + ía, ías, ía, íamos, íais, ían

Example: hablaría, comería, viviría

Gustar & Similar Verbs

Structure
me gusta(n), te gusta(n), le gusta(n), nos gusta(n), os gusta(n), les gusta(n)

Use "gusta" for singular/infinitives, "gustan" for plurals

Similar verbs
encantar (to love), fascinar (to fascinate), interesar (to interest), importar (to matter), apetecer (to feel like)

Por vs Para

POR (through, because of, by)
Duration: por tres días | Reason: por el calor | Exchange: por 10 euros | Means: por teléfono
PARA (for, in order to)
Purpose: para estudiar | Destination: para Madrid | Deadline: para mañana | Recipient: para ti

Ser vs Estar

SER (permanent/identity)
Identity: Soy irlandés | Time: Son las tres | Characteristics: Es inteligente | Origin: Es de Madrid
ESTAR (temporary/location)
Location: Estoy en casa | Condition: Estoy cansado | Mood: Está contento | Progressive: Estoy estudiando

Mark Scheme Decoder

Understanding how Spanish examiners award marks is crucial for maximising your score.

General Marking Rules

RuleDetail
Answer in correct languageQuestions specify "in Spanish" or "in English". Wrong language = zero marks for that question.
Exact transcription requiredFor "write words from text" questions, you must copy exactly. No extra words or omissions allowed.
Verbs must be correctIn written production sections, incorrect verb forms lose marks even if the message is understood.
No copying from textsIn Section B Q5 (opinion writing), phrases taken directly from the reading text earn no marks.
Synonyms acceptedAnswers equivalent or synonymous with marking scheme answers are acceptable unless exact transcription is required.
First correct answer ruleIn some multi-part questions, the first correct answer gets more marks (e.g., 4m) than subsequent answers (3m each).

Section A - Reading Comprehension (70 marks)

Q1(a) Prescribed Literature (50 marks) - Relato de un náufrago by García Márquez. Questions test vocabulary extraction (3+3+3), English explanations (3+3+3), character details (4+3+3), description (4+3+3), and broader discussion (12 marks).
Q1(b) Journalistic Text (50 marks) - Cultural article "¡España es diferente!". English comprehension (5×4 = 20m), Spanish vocabulary extraction (3×4 = 12m), English explanations (3×4 = 12m), Spanish rephrasing (6m).
Q2 Short Articles (20 marks) - Two articles (10 marks each). Questions require specific details in English. Each sub-question worth 2-4 marks.

Section B - AI Influencers Text (100 marks)

Q1 Spanish vocabulary extraction (15 marks) - 5 phrases worth 3 marks each. Must write exact phrases from text that match the meaning given.
Q2 English explanations (15 marks) - 3 phrases worth 5 marks each. Explain the meaning in context, not word-for-word translation.
Q3 Spanish synonyms (5 marks) - 5 single words worth 1 mark each. Find one-word equivalents from the text.
Q4 English summary (15 marks) - Part (a): Give 3 details about Alba's popularity (2+2+2 = 6m, first answer gets 1 bonus). Part (b): Give 3 expenses not relevant to Alba (2+2+2 = 6m). Part (c): Explain opinion (3m).
Q5 Opinion writing (50 marks) - 80-150 words in Spanish. Marked as Content/Communication (25m) and Language (25m). Choose from 3 topics.

Q2(a) Diary Entry (20 marks) - 4 points worth 5 marks each. Development not essential but helps secure marks.
Q2(b) Note (20 marks) - 4 points worth 5 marks each. Brief but clear communication required.

Listening Comprehension (80 marks)

All answers in English. If entire test answered in Spanish, marks are halved.
Q1 Silent Disco Tour (9m) - 3m for new experience description, 2+1m for two reasons it's special, 3m for occasions.
Q2 Almodóvar Interview (12m) - 4 questions worth 3m each. Full details required where specified.
Q3 Paula Romaguera Interview (13m) - 3m + 3m + 4m + (2+1)m structure. First correct answer in multi-part worth more.
Q4 Birth Month (13m) - 3m + 3m + (2+2)m + 3m structure.
Q5 Bernabéu Concerts (13m) - 4m + 2m + (2+1)m + 4m structure.
Q6 Weather (8m) - 2m + 3m + 3m for specific weather details.
Q7 Maori Clan (12m) - 4m + 3m + 2m + 3m structure with full details required.

Golden Rules for Maximum Marks

1. Check the language required. "In Spanish" vs "in English" is clearly marked. Don't lose easy marks by answering in the wrong language.
2. Use correct verb tenses. Present, preterite, future, conditional - use the tense that matches the situation. Wrong tenses = lost marks.
3. Develop your written responses. One-sentence answers in dialogues/letters rarely earn full marks. Expand each point.
4. Don't copy from texts in opinion writing. Paraphrase and use your own words to express ideas.
5. Listen three times strategically. First: understand context. Second: write answers. Third: verify details and spelling.
6. Give full details when asked. "Give full details" means multiple elements. Don't stop at one fact.
7. Write clearly within answer boxes. Anything outside the boxes may not be scanned or seen by examiners.

Section A - Reading Comprehension (70 marks)

Question 1 requires you to answer EITHER (a) Prescribed Literature OR (b) Journalistic Text. Choose your best option.

Q1(a) Prescribed Literature: Relato de un náufrago

Extract about Luis Alejandro Velasco experiencing hallucinations on a raft. He dreams of being in Mobile, Alabama at a café with the Jewish shopkeeper Massey Nasser, then suddenly sees an enormous yellow turtle.

Sample Questions:
Q1: Find words in text meaning:
  • (a) "de nuevo" (again) → Answer: otra vez (3 marks)
  • (b) "observé" (I observed) → Answer: vi (3 marks)
  • (c) "un corto tiempo" (a short time) → Answer: breves minutos (3 marks)
Q2: Explain phrases in English:
  • (a) "no comprábamos en un almacén distinto al suyo" → "we used not buy in a different shop to his" (3m)
  • (b) "el rostro cubierto por un velo" → "the/her face covered by a veil, like the Arabic dancers" (3m)
  • (c) "de pronto di un salto en la balsa" → "suddenly I jumped (up) in the raft" (3m)
Q3: What do we find out about Massey Nasser? (Give 3 details, first worth 4m, next two worth 3m each)
  • He was Jewish
  • Shop assistant in Mobile
  • He had given Luis Alejandro the business cards
  • He served the Colombian sailors
  • He spoke Spanish correctly despite never being in a Spanish-speaking country
  • He was the happiest at the open-air café
  • He sold fine and cheap clothes to Colombian sailors

Pick your best 3. First correct answer = 4 marks, others = 3 marks each.

Q4: How does Luis describe the turtle? (Give 3 details, first worth 4m)
  • Yellow, enormous, striped head
  • Fixed and inexpressive eyes
  • Eyes like two giant crystal balls
  • Monstrous
  • Scary, painted head, like from a nightmare
  • Measured about four metres from head to tail
  • Didn't know if it was reality or fantasy
Q5: Discuss the life of a sailor as described in the novel (12 marks)

This requires you to discuss broader themes from the book - NOT just the extract. You might mention:

  • Long periods at sea away from family
  • Danger and unpredictability
  • Camaraderie among crew members
  • Extreme survival situations
  • Military discipline and routine

Important: No marks for answers referencing the extract. This tests your knowledge of the whole book.

Q5: Opinion Writing (50 marks)

Write 80-150 words in Spanish on ONE of these topics:

  • (a) Las redes sociales son más interesantes que la vida real (Social media is more interesting than real life)
  • (b) La inteligencia artificial tiene la capacidad de hacerlo todo (AI has the capacity to do everything)
  • (c) Los trabajos tradicionales son los más importantes (Traditional jobs are the most important)
Marking: Content/Communication (25m) + Language (25m). Top band (18-25) requires high coherence, clear argumentation, idiomatic Spanish, good vocabulary, correct verb tenses.
Sample structure for topic (b):

En mi opinión, aunque la inteligencia artificial es muy poderosa, no tiene la capacidad de hacerlo todo. Es verdad que la IA puede realizar muchas tareas de manera eficiente, como el análisis de datos o la creación de contenido básico. Sin embargo, hay aspectos de la vida humana que la IA nunca podrá replicar completamente.

Por ejemplo, la creatividad genuina, la empatía y las conexiones emocionales requieren experiencia humana. Además, la IA depende de los datos y algoritmos creados por humanos, por lo tanto siempre necesitará supervisión humana.

En conclusión, la IA es una herramienta muy útil, pero no puede sustituir completamente el talento y la inteligencia humana.

(120 words - demonstrates clear structure, opinion, examples, and conclusion)

Q1(a): Dialogue about Erasmus (30 marks)

Complete a conversation with Jorge about his Erasmus scholarship plans. 5 turns worth 6 marks each.

Sample responses:

Turn 6: "¿Te gustaría salir el fin de semana para celebrar? Hay un concierto en el estadio el sábado y muchos de mis amigos van a estar allí."

Q1(b): Letter about over-tourism in Spain (30 marks)

Write a letter/email to a Spanish newspaper editor about over-tourism. Make 5 developed points from the 8 suggested (or your own relevant points).

Sample letter opening:

Estimado señor/a,

Le escribo en respuesta a su artículo reciente sobre el turismo excesivo en España. Como irlandesa que visita España frecuentemente, tengo opiniones fuertes sobre este tema importante.

Por un lado, estoy de acuerdo en que muchos españoles sienten que el turismo está fuera de control. Los visitantes extranjeros crean muchos empleos en la industria hotelera y de servicios, lo cual beneficia la economía española enormemente. Sin embargo, algunos turistas extranjeros causan problemas, especialmente en ciudades como Barcelona y Madrid donde el ruido y el comportamiento irrespetuoso afectan negativamente a los residentes locales.

Además, creo firmemente que la gente local debe tener acceso a vivienda asequible. Es injusto que los precios de alquiler suban tanto que los españoles no puedan vivir en sus propias ciudades debido a los apartamentos turísticos.

Atentamente,

Section B Reading Text: Los 'Influencers' de IA

Full text about AI influencers with detailed analysis.

Full Text Translation

Paragraph 1: It's not surprising that many young Spanish people aspire to be influencers in fashion, beauty, lifestyle or wellness. Today there are more than 235,000 influencers in Spain, and some Spanish universities offer Influencer Courses. However, it seems that soon the figure of the human influencer could become an endangered species, because influencers generated by Artificial Intelligence (AI) are already among us and are beginning to have a significant impact, both on social media and in the real world.

Paragraph 2: At the forefront of this revolution in Spain is Alba Renai (@albarenai), an AI figure created by a Madrid marketing agency. Alba is 24 years old and lives in Madrid. She is very pretty, elegant and very nice. She is passionate about travel and fashion. She is becoming very popular in Spain. She has thousands of followers on Instagram and is the face of a marketing campaign for the hotel company Meliá. In 2024 Alba became the first AI influencer to present a Spanish television program, Supervivientes. She also participated in the GenZ 2023 awards gala.

Paragraph 3: Luis Movilla, spokesperson for Alba's inventors, explains the process of creating Alba: "she was created completely with Artificial Intelligence. First we developed a test model with ChatGPT and introduced information about social media studies, reports on behaviors and tastes of Generation Z. We also studied the results of a survey of about three hundred young people about personality attributes, tastes and physical appearance that they most value." Then with other AI tools her physical appearance was developed: the face, her skin textures, clothes, hair and the melody of her voice. Alba seems like something from a science fiction film.

Paragraph 4: After creating the physical person, it was necessary to give her personality and values. The University of Málaga helped with this, having developed an ethical code focused on the behavior of virtual influencers. Begoña Soto from the University of Málaga comments: "with the use of this code we ensure that Alba not only had an extroverted and cheerful personality but also an inclusive, diverse and supportive vision of the world, that respects all cultures."

Paragraph 5: Movilla makes known the problems of working with some traditional human influencers. "They can be busy or feel too tired to create content. In the worst case, they can express offensive opinions or have inappropriate behaviors that negatively affect the image of the brand they represent." Influencers are also not cheap, in Spain, the most sought-after charge about eight thousand euros per publication.

Paragraph 6: On the other hand, Alba has the capacity to travel from one place to another instantly, or to be in two places at once. Alba doesn't suppose any significant expense; in reality not even a single euro is spent on wardrobe, accommodation, makeup, hairdressing and other costs related to human influencers. She's always in a good mood and can anticipate the needs of brands.

Paragraph 7: Taking into account the very clear advantages of AI influencers, many have commented that this is going to take away work from a lot of people. How does Movilla respond to the idea that they could soon completely replace millions of human workers in this sector? "I don't see it like that, because AI influencers are the result of the effort of human professionals like engineers and digital marketing specialists," opines Movilla. The truth is that artificial intelligence has given work opportunities to many people, but at the end of the day human talent is irreplaceable.

Prescribed Literature: Relato de un náufrago

Author: Gabriel García Márquez

Full Title: Relato de un náufrago que estuvo diez días en una balsa sin comer ni beber, que fue proclamado héroe de la patria, besado por las reinas de la belleza y hecho rico por la publicidad, y luego aborrecido por el gobierno y olvidado para siempre

About the Book

Based on the true story of Luis Alejandro Velasco, a Colombian sailor who survived 10 days alone on a raft in the Caribbean Sea in 1955 after falling overboard from a destroyer. García Márquez interviewed Velasco and wrote the story as a first-person narrative for a newspaper series in 1955, which was later published as a book in 1970.

Key themes: Survival, solitude, hallucinations due to hunger/thirst, government corruption (the destroyer was illegally carrying contraband), media manipulation, the nature of heroism.

Extract Analysis (2025 Exam)

The extract shows Luis experiencing hallucinations on the raft. He dreams of being back in Mobile, Alabama at an outdoor café with Massey Nasser, the Jewish shopkeeper who sold clothes to Colombian sailors. This represents his mind trying to escape the harsh reality of his situation.

The appearance of the enormous yellow turtle (whether real or hallucination) shows the disorientation and uncertainty of his condition - "Yo no sabía si era realidad o fantasía" (I didn't know if it was reality or fantasy).

Key vocabulary from extract:

  • náufrago - castaway/shipwreck survivor
  • balsa - raft
  • destructor - destroyer (warship)
  • marinero - sailor
  • dependiente - shop assistant
  • almacén - store/warehouse
  • embotado - dazed/numb
  • alucinación - hallucination
  • atardeciendo - getting dark/dusk
  • tortuga - turtle
  • atigrada - striped/tiger-striped
  • monstruoso - monstrous
  • aterrorizado - terrified
  • espuma - foam
  • pesadilla - nightmare

Study Tips for Prescribed Literature

1. Know the whole book, not just extracts. Q5 asks you to discuss broader themes (12 marks) and specifically says no marks for referencing the extract.
2. Practice vocabulary extraction. Q1 worth 9 marks tests your ability to find exact phrases with specific meanings.
3. Understand character relationships. Who is Massey Nasser? Why is he important to Luis? What does he represent?
4. Analyze descriptive language. How does García Márquez describe the turtle? What literary devices are used?
5. Themes to discuss for Q5: Survival instinct, government corruption, media sensationalism, the fragility of fame, human resilience, solitude, hallucinations vs reality.
